Summary:

Titanic is an epic romance and disaster film directed, written, co-produced, and co-edited by James Cameron. It revolves around the love story between Jack Dawson, a poor artist, and Rose DeWitt Bukater, a young woman from a wealthy family, aboard the ill-fated RMS Titanic during its maiden voyage from Southampton to New York City.

Amidst the luxurious backdrop of the "ship of dreams," Jack and Rose's love flourishes, but their happiness is short-lived as the Titanic strikes an iceberg and starts sinking. The movie beautifully captures the tragic events of the ship's sinking, while the love story remains at the heart of the film.

Titanic was a critical and commercial success, winning 11 Academy Awards, including Best Picture and Best Director. It became one of the highest-grossing films of all time, leaving a lasting impact on cinema history.
